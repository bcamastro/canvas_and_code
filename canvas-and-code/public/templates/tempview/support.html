Areas of support for women:

Reproductive and Maternal Mental Health

Trying to conceive, coping with loss, or adjusting to pregnancy and postpartum can bring emotional highs and lows. Therapy offers space to process fertility challenges, miscarriage, or pregnancy termination and to explore the complex mix of grief, joy, and change with compassion and without judgment.

Motherhood and Caregiving

Motherhood transforms everything, your time, your relationships, and your sense of self. Whether you’re adjusting to life with a new baby, navigating the ups and downs of parenting, or caring for aging parents, it’s easy to lose yourself along the way. Together, we’ll help you find your footing and make space for yourself again..

Mental and Emotional Wellness

When stress, burnout, or anxiety start to take over, it’s easy to lose your sense of self. Maybe you’re running on empty, snapping more than you’d like, or just not feeling like you. In therapy, we’ll slow things down, make sense of what’s happening, and help you feel more grounded and at ease.

Neurodiversity and Overwhelm

If you’ve wondered whether you might have ADHD, or if you’ve long felt scattered, distracted, or constantly behind, you’re not alone. We’ll look at your patterns with curiosity and compassion, finding ways to work with your brain, not against it. Together, we’ll create strategies that align with your strengths and bring greater calm and confidence.

Relationships and Relational Support

Relationships can be complicated, even with the people we love most. You might feel disconnected from your partner, family, or friends, or unsure how to express what you need. In therapy, we’ll explore the patterns that create distance or tension and strengthen communication, boundaries, and connection with others and with yourself.

Loss and Grief

Loss comes in many forms, a person, a relationship, a place, or even a version of yourself. Sometimes the pain lingers because we’ve never had space to fully feel it. In therapy, you don’t have to hold it together. We’ll make room for the sadness and help you begin to find meaning and healing.

Life Transitions and Identity

Life has a way of shifting when you least expect it. Marriage, divorce, career changes, or new chapters can leave you questioning who you are and what’s next. Therapy offers a place to explore who you’re becoming and clarify what truly matters to you.

Midlife, Aging, and Purpose

Midlife can bring unexpected questions and change. You might notice shifts in your body or hormones, feel restless in your routines, or wonder what’s next. It’s natural to want something deeper. In therapy, we’ll explore what’s changing, reconnect with your purpose, and help you feel more centered and fulfilled.

Frequently Asked Questions

I offer teletherapy (video or phone) because it fits with the realities of modern life, especially for women and mothers. Whether you’re working, managing a household, caring for kids, or juggling all of it, having the flexibility to log in from wherever you are can make all the difference. Many women tell me they were unsure about teletherapy at first but ended up feeling just as seen and supported as they would in person. Some even say they’re more comfortable talking from their own space, whether that’s a home office, a parked car, or while walking outside. And yes, plenty of clients use their phones during sessions whatever works. I also understand that life doesn’t always fit neatly into a 45-50 minute session at a desk or in a quiet space. Many of my clients join by phone while taking a walk, hopping on the treadmill, sitting in their car, or even with little ones in the background. I’m flexible in how we work together, so you can get the support you need without having to wait for “perfect” conditions. If you’re not sure whether teletherapy is right for you, that’s okay. We can talk about it and figure out what feels doable and supportive for your life right now.

I’m an out-of-network provider, but I handle things a little differently. I’ll check your benefits for you and let you know up front whether using your insurance makes sense based on what your plan actually covers. I don’t expect you to figure out the claims process on your own. I take care of that by submitting everything directly, which makes reimbursement a lot easier. I know most people, especially mothers, don’t have time to chase paperwork, and I want therapy to feel like one less thing you have to manage. Sometimes, depending on your plan, you may only need to pay a portion up front, and I’ll wait for the rest to come in from your insurance company. Every situation is different, and if your plan doesn’t offer much, I’ll still walk you through your options and help you make a plan.

I’m not the kind of therapist who just nods and waits for you to talk. I’m fully present and engaged in our conversations, asking questions, getting curious, and really trying to understand what’s going on beneath the surface. I use a mix of approaches depending on what works for you, including psychodynamic therapy, cognitive behavioral therapy, dialectical behavioral therapy, and the teaching of mindfulness. Once we get to know each other, I will tailor our work to what will be most beneficial for you. We’ll talk about your unique challenges, what’s worked (or hasn’t) in past therapy, and decide together which strategies or combination of approaches make the most sense moving forward. It’s a collaborative process every step of the way.

After our first session, most sessions are between 45 and 50 minutes. That said, I’ll never cut you off mid-thought or rush you out. We always take a few minutes to wrap up and figure out what feels important to carry into the next week. If you ever feel like the time goes too fast, we can talk about ways to extend sessions or adjust things. It’s your time, and I want it to feel like it’s working for you.

I don’t offer a full consultation session for free, but I’m happy to do a short 15 minute phone call if you have questions or you’re unsure about getting started. If we decide to move forward, we’ll schedule a paid initial session. There’s no pressure to commit to anything beyond that it’s just a chance to see how it feels to talk and go from there.

If you’re ready to move forward, we’ll find a time that works for both of us and get paperwork out of the way ahead of time so that when we meet, it’s just about you not the logistics. This first session is a chance to get to know each other and see what it’s like to work together. You don’t need to have anything perfectly figured out before we start. Just come as you are, and we’ll take it from there.
